# **Memo to Finance Head**



**Subject:**

&#x20;       Bank Reconciliation Review -- Jan to Mar 2026.

A reconciliation was performed between the Zoho Books ledger and the
HDFC Bank statement for the period 01-Jan-2026 to 31-Mar-2026.



**Findings:**

&#x20;        The largest discrepancy relates to an opening balance mismatch
of ₹7,850.50, indicating that the prior-period reconciliation was not
fully resolved. In addition, several bank-originated transactions were
not recorded in the accounting system. These include interest credits
for January, February, and March and quarterly maintenance charges
debited by the bank.

Operational discrepancies were also identified. One internet expense
entry for ACT Fibernet was recorded twice in Zoho but appears only once
in the bank statement. Additionally, a vendor payment to Packaging
Solutions was recorded with an incorrect amount compared to the bank
debit. A standing instruction rent payment of ₹45,000 was automatically
debited by the bank but was not entered in the accounting records.

Three items were classified as timing differences rather than errors.
These involve cheque deposits recorded in Zoho before clearing in the
bank and one cheque issued near the end of March that had not yet been
presented to the bank.



**Root Cause:**

&#x20;         The discrepancies primarily arise from manual bookkeeping
and the absence of automated bank feeds. When bank-originated entries
such as charges or interest are not automatically imported, they must be
manually recorded, increasing the risk of omission. Additionally, lack
of periodic reconciliation allows small errors to accumulate.



**Recommendations:**

&#x20;            1. Perform a formal bank reconciliation every month rather than quarterly.

&#x20;            2. Enable automated bank feeds between HDFC Bank and Zoho Books to capture bank-originated transactions.

&#x20;            3. Implement a review workflow for manual expense entries to prevent duplicates or incorrect amounts.

&#x20;            4. Maintain a reconciliation checklist at month-end to ensure all bank adjustments are recorded before closing the books.
